GLOBAL

1. Trump Media Enters Crypto with New CRO Venture

Trump Media & Technology Group has partnered with Crypto.com and Yorkville Acquisition to launch Trump Media Group CRO Strategy, a new company that will acquire and hold CRO tokens, the native cryptocurrency of the Cronos blockchain. The new entity aims to raise billions to build what it calls the "first and largest publicly traded CRO treasury company." In a separate agreement, the CRO token will also be integrated into the Truth Social platform as part of a new rewards system. This latest move by the Trump family into the crypto space comes amid growing concerns about potential conflicts of interest.

2. The AI Race Is Slowing Down, And That Could Be Good News

The rapid advancement of large language models, which has fueled intense excitement and investment in the tech industry since the launch of ChatGPT, appears to be leveling off. Companies like Meta and OpenAI have reportedly struggled to significantly improve their latest models, leading to delays and tempered expectations. However, this slowdown may not be a bad thing for businesses. While generative AI is already a powerful tool for tasks like summarizing text and writing code, most companies have only just begun to integrate the technology. A slower pace of innovation could give businesses the time they need to figure out how to best implement and trust current AI tools, a process that is often more of a management challenge than a technical one. This shift in pace could also provide more stability for the market, allowing companies to invest in the technology with more confidence.

INDIA

1. District Cooling Systems: A Cool Idea with Major Obstacles

District cooling systems (DCS), which use a centralized plant to cool a neighborhood, are being explored in India to combat rising electricity demand from AC use. While highly efficient due to their scale, implementing them widely faces significant challenges. The high cost of underground piping, suitability only for densely populated areas, and a reliance on India's still carbon-heavy power grid make it less of a silver bullet. While DCS could be a solution for new, planned business districts, it is unlikely to replace the millions of individual AC units in residential homes. The ultimate success of DCS in India will hinge on proactive government policy and city-wide planning rather than technology alone.

2. Gaming in India Shifts Focus from Real-Money to AAA Titles

Following the new law that bans real-money games (RMGs), India’s gaming industry is at a crossroads. While the law aims to protect citizens, it has effectively halted a sector that made up 85% of the market. This shift has forced developers to focus on building large-scale, high-budget "AAA" games, similar to global hits like Grand Theft Auto. However, this presents a new set of challenges, including a need for patient, long-term capital, and the development of homegrown intellectual property. With government policies now supporting the broader gaming ecosystem, India’s talent may finally get the chance to move beyond outsourcing and create its own world-class games.

3. ONGC Plans New Trading Unit for Oil and Gas

State-run Oil and Natural Gas Corp. (ONGC) is in the early stages of creating a new trading unit for crude oil, petroleum products, and petrochemicals. According to Rajarshi Gupta, the managing director of ONGC Videsh, this new group would help manage the significant volatility and uncertainty in the global energy market. The new unit would consolidate the buying and selling of crude oil for ONGC and its subsidiaries, including Hindustan Petroleum Corp. (HPCL) and Mangalore Refinery and Petrochemicals Ltd (MRPL), which together handle around 100 million tonnes of crude oil annually. While still in the conceptual phase, the company believes this move is essential for India's growing economy and rising energy demands.
