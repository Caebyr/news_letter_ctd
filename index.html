GLOBAL

1. Paramount-Skydance Merger Fuels Streaming Growth and Strategic Expansion

Paramount reported steady third-quarter results in its first earnings since merging with David Ellison’s Skydance Media, highlighting a 17% year-over-year surge in direct-to-consumer revenue driven by Paramount+. Total revenue held flat at $6.7 billion, with projections set at $30 billion for 2026. The company expects its streaming arm to turn profitable by 2025, with a U.S. price hike for Paramount+ slated for early 2026. Meanwhile, TV Media revenue dropped 12% amid lower ad sales. Since the merger, Ellison has pursued aggressive moves, including a $7.7 billion UFC rights deal, a $150 million acquisition of The Free Press, and multiple bids for Warner Bros. Discovery. Paramount also raised its cost-cutting goal to $3 billion and announced layoffs affecting 2,000 employees. Despite turbulence, Paramount+ now boasts 79.1 million subscribers, signaling strong momentum in the evolving media landscape.

2. Buffett Reaffirms Faith in Successor Greg Abel, Pledges to Retain Shares Until Transition Stabilizes

In a heartfelt “Thanksgiving message” to shareholders and his children, Warren Buffett announced he will retain a substantial portion of his Berkshire Hathaway Class A shares until investors grow confident in his successor, Greg Abel. Buffett, 95, emphasized Abel’s integrity, work ethic, and leadership as he prepares to assume the CEO role at year’s end, while Buffett remains chairman. The legendary investor also revealed a $1.3 billion donation—1,800 Class A shares converted into 2.7 million Class B shares—distributed among four family foundations. As part of the succession, Abel will take over Berkshire’s iconic annual letter and lead future shareholder meetings. Despite investor unease and an “underperform” rating last month, Berkshire’s strong Q3 results—highlighted by a 17% earnings rise and record $358 billion in cash reserves—underscore the company’s enduring strength as it transitions into the post-Buffett era.

3. Sony Lifts Forecast as Tariff Impact Eases and Core Businesses Strengthen

Sony Group has raised its full-year earnings outlook after reporting a stronger-than-expected second quarter, powered by growth in its music and image-sensor divisions. The company’s net profit climbed 6.7% year-over-year to ¥311.4 billion ($2.02 billion), surpassing analyst forecasts, while revenue rose 4.6% to ¥3.1 trillion. Sony now expects U.S. tariffs to trim operating profit by ¥50 billion this fiscal year, down from the ¥70 billion impact it previously projected. The electronics and entertainment giant also forecasts annual net profit of ¥1.05 trillion and revenue of ¥12 trillion. Shares jumped 5.5% following the results, adding to a 32% year-to-date rally fueled by optimism around Sony’s gaming and entertainment pivot. After spinning off its financial arm, Sony has doubled down on content expansion through major investments in Kadokawa and Bandai Namco, while raising PlayStation 5 prices amid a tightening global economy.

INDIA

1. Tata Steel Faces Pivotal Quarter as Europe Turnaround and India Growth Take Center Stage

Tata Steel’s upcoming Q2 results mark a crucial test for CEO T.V. Narendran’s dual mission: reviving European profitability and scaling India’s capacity toward the ambitious 40 million tonnes per annum target by 2030. Investor sentiment has brightened with the stock up 18.5% since April and a €2 billion financing boost from the Dutch government for decarbonizing its IJmuiden plant—though final approval remains pending. In Europe, analysts expect improved margins from lower raw material costs and stronger performance in the Netherlands, but continued challenges in the UK could delay breakeven to FY26. In India, ramp-ups at Kalinganagar and NINL remain key to reaching 31–32 mtpa capacity. Analysts forecast Q2 revenue at ₹57,640 crore, up 7% year-on-year, with Ebitda margins rising to 14.2%. Market watchers will also eye demand recovery, price trends, and clarity on new import duties as Tata Steel navigates a transformative phase in its global strategy.

2. PhysicsWallah IPO Opens to Tepid Start as Investors Weigh Growth Potential Against Profit Risks

Edtech unicorn PhysicsWallah opened its much-anticipated ₹3,480 crore IPO on November 11, with early subscriptions reaching only 3% as of midday. The price band is set at ₹103–₹109 per share, and the grey market premium (GMP) has slipped to ₹3, indicating a modest 2–3% expected listing gain. The offering comprises ₹3,100 crore in fresh equity and a ₹380 crore offer-for-sale from founders Alakh Pandey and Prateek Boob. Despite robust revenue growth—rising to ₹2,887 crore in FY25 from ₹1,941 crore—and sharply reduced losses to ₹243 crore, analysts remain cautious. Angel One issued a “Neutral” rating, citing ongoing profitability concerns and high operating costs amid intense competition. The IPO aims to fund new offline and hybrid centers, cloud infrastructure, and marketing expansion. Backed by major funds including Goldman Sachs and Fidelity, PhysicsWallah’s listing on November 18 will serve as a key test for India’s recovering edtech sector.

3. ChatGPT said: FASTag Data Becomes the Engine of India’s Highway Reform and Smart Mobility

What began as a tool to ease toll payments has evolved into a data-driven revolution in India’s highway governance. With over 80 million users and ₹70,000 crore in annual collections, FASTag now powers real-time insights for the Ministry of Road Transport and NHAI to plan new corridors, manage congestion, and reform tolling systems. The data—covering vehicle types, routes, and travel frequency—helps identify bottlenecks, improve traffic flow, and rationalize project investments. It’s also reviving private participation in highway development by providing accurate traffic projections, a key gap that crippled earlier BOT projects. FASTag analytics is enabling pilots in dynamic tolling, distance-based pricing, and off-peak travel discounts. Integrated with e-challan and Vahan databases, it also enhances enforcement and safety. Future reforms include GPS-based tolling, digital corridors, and open data platforms—transforming FASTag into the backbone of India’s intelligent transport network.
