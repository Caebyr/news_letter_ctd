GLOBAL

1. Tech Titans Pour $45 Billion Into Anthropic to Supercharge AI Race

Microsoft, Nvidia, and Anthropic have locked in a massive $45 billion partnership that cements their roles at the center of the AI arms race. Nvidia will invest up to $10 billion and Microsoft up to $5 billion in Anthropic, while Anthropic commits to buying $30 billion worth of compute on Microsoft’s Azure—plus an extra gigawatt of Nvidia’s advanced systems. The three companies will also co-engineer future hardware and software, making each other both partners and customers as they scale. Anthropic’s Claude models will now run on Azure, giving Microsoft broader AI options beyond its heavy OpenAI stake. The deal showcases Big Tech’s rapid infrastructure buildup, even as questions linger over whether the flood of investment will pay off.

2. Markets Slide as Fear of an AI Bubble and Weak Retail Outlook Rattle Investors

U.S. markets fell for a fourth straight session, with heavy losses hitting both high-growth tech names and traditional retailers. Chip stocks dragged the Nasdaq lower as investors grew uneasy about soaring valuations and the massive debt companies are taking on to fund AI infrastructure, with nearly half of fund managers now calling an AI bubble the top risk to markets. Home Depot’s reduced outlook added pressure to the Dow, while global markets from Japan to Europe also sank. Treasury yields eased for a second day and Bitcoin briefly dipped below $90,000, underscoring a broader pullback as investors brace for upcoming labor data.

3. Google Fires Back in the AI Race With the Launch of Gemini 3

Google rolled out Gemini 3, a major upgrade aimed at narrowing the gap with OpenAI and reclaiming ground in the chatbot race. The new model promises sharper answers, stronger coding and app-building skills, and faster, more realistic image generation—building on the explosive success of Google’s Nano Banana tool, which has already produced over five billion images. With Gemini usage climbing and a revamped app interface, Google is leaning on its scale: custom chips, dominance in search, and a massive product ecosystem now infused with AI. The company is also introducing advanced features for subscribers, including an agent that can handle multistep tasks and a new developer platform. Whether Gemini 3 can truly challenge ChatGPT remains to be seen, but Google is clearly betting big on catching up.

INDIA

1. Sitharaman Opens Pre-Budget Talks With Industry, Spotlight on Innovation and Capital Access

Finance minister Nirmala Sitharaman met leaders from capital markets, startups, and manufacturing as part of her latest pre-budget consultation, with discussions centered on boosting innovation, widening access to capital, easing regulatory choke points, and supporting job creation. Industry representatives pushed for stronger credit flows, better digital infrastructure, tighter fintech oversight, and a cut in Securities Transaction Tax to improve market efficiency. The meeting is one of several the ministry is holding across sectors as it shapes the 2026–27 budget, aiming to balance welfare with growth by encouraging private investment while supporting agriculture, MSMEs, and broader economic resilience.

2. Global PE Giants Circle $1 Billion ValueLabs Buyout as Investor Interest in Enterprise Tech Surges

EQT, PAG, Blackstone, and CVC are among the major private equity firms evaluating a $1 billion bid for Hyderabad-based software services firm ValueLabs, reflecting the rising appetite for mid-sized Indian enterprise tech companies. Due diligence is underway, with first-round bids expected by the end of November, and promoters likely to retain a minority stake. ValueLabs—an agentic AI and digital engineering player with 7,000+ engineers and 300 clients—has drawn attention despite modest FY24 revenue and profit dips. The interest aligns with a broader trend of global funds buying into IT and AI-driven service providers seen as stable, high-growth bets in a transforming tech landscape.

3. Google Pledges Full Data Localization in India as It Launches Gemini 3

Google unveiled Gemini 3 with a clear commitment to store all AI usage data within India, a move driven by regulatory pressure and essential for winning over enterprise and public-sector clients. The company is preparing full data-residency support as it rolls out its most advanced AI models across search, the Gemini app, developer tools, and Vertex AI. With Indian heavyweights like TCS, Reliance, Apollo Hospitals, and Myntra already on board, Google is positioning itself as a serious challenger to OpenAI—whose ChatGPT has also begun partial localization. Analysts say tight data control and rapid model upgrades will be decisive as Big Tech battles for dominance in India’s fast-emerging enterprise AI market.
